What Cursor, Antigravity, and ChatGPT Think of Me

2026-02-18

I did a lot of prompting today, so I thought I'd get a little self-reflective. I'm still not sure if they've fixed the glazing problem, but here was my prompt:

If you're able to access all our chats today, please give me a 1–2 page summary of everything we worked on today and what you thought about working with me. What we learned. Anything we got stuck on. It's for my dev blog but I want your opinion.

It gave me a long and surprisingly good summary of my day. Also, I think I have psychosis now. So, enjoy!

Cursor said I give clear context (terminal output, exact errors) so bugs are easier to find. I also push for the right fix, not just a passing test, and I care about how things feel for users and what gets shown in docs.

Antigravity called me a "High-Velocity Visionary." I switch between big-picture design and small details without losing track. I ask why something broke and how to make the fix solid, not just "make it work."

ChatGPT said I think in systems and edge cases. I move fast, so I run into more integration issues in a day than some do in a week. In short: builder brain with architect instincts, and right now I'm stress-testing how far the infrastructure can go.

I'm smitten, Sam take all my money! Just kidding. The tl;dr is that I'm getting a lot out of these $20/month subscriptions and I think as long as they keep offering them I'm going to keep bouncing between girlfriends... I mean agents.

I typically stick to one agent type per project which has the added bonus of keeping my global conversation history focused!

Project bleedover is a major issue, especially when you have multiple clients... a topic for next time maybe.
